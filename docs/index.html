SuperSelect

Select and save text from the web with speed.

SuperSelect is a Chrome browser extension, but you can use some of its features right here on this web page to learn how it works!

At the bottom right of this screen you will see the SuperSelect controls.

These controls can be available on all web pages that you visit, once the SuperSelect extension is installed.

The Overlay

The SuperSelect Overlay is like a piece of glass laid over the current web page. To turn it On, we simply press the "Z" key. We could also click in the controls, but the keyboard is faster.

Let's enable the Overlay now. Press the "Z" key. You should see the tinted Overlay and a blue border appear. Pressing the "Z" key again should toggle the Overlay Off.

Consider pressing the "Z" a thousand times until you understand how this works :)

Selecting Stuff

Once the Overlay is On selecting text is just a matter of clicking the start and end positions of the text you wish to select.

Your Try

One trivial use case for SimpleSelect is collecting information to pull into a flash card application such as Anki.

For example, let's say we want to collect words from a dictionary site to add to our ever-growing personal vocabulary flash card deck. Here's a word you might like:

mondegreen
noun; mon-di-green
a misinterpretation of a word or phrase that has been heard, especially a song lyric.
Example
"There's a bathroom on the right" is a mondegreen of Creedence Clearwater Revival's "There's a bad moon on the rise."

Let's grab this word and its data using SuperSelect.

First, make sure the Overlay is enabled. Press "Z" until the blue border appears. Then click on the word "mondegreen" once and then once again. Then click on the word "a" right before "misinterpretation" and complete the selection by clicking on "lyric".

The Buffer

As you make selections you'll see that they appear above the SuperSelect control. This is called the Buffer. All selections appear in the Buffer until the Buffer is saved by clicking on the icon or pressing the "S" key.

At any time you can remove selections from your Buffer by clicking the appropriate icon.

The Workspace

This is a demo, so Save doesn't work here to actually store your selections.

The extension however, when installed, will persist all of your Saved selections to a backend spreadsheet table, called the Workspace, where the data can be edited and exported to any application you like.

Each Save of the Buffer would create a new row in the backend spreadsheet table.
